Program Description

The Business Technology Management (BTM) program at Canada’s largest institution for business and ICT education combines technology, business, and management principles. The program emphasizes a deep understanding of business fundamentals while driving technology-based innovation through case studies, hands-on lab experiences.

Relevant Coursework:

  1. IT Infrastructure: Introduces computer hardware, software, and networks, fostering effective communication with IT professionals.

  2. Network Administration: Explores foundational information handling, emphasizing hands-on skills in network administration. Gained hands-on experience with Linux.

  3. Information Systems Security and Privacy: Addresses technical, operational, and managerial aspects of security, incorporating industry best practices for information systems.

  4. Foundations of Information Systems: Introduces contemporary information systems, emphasizing troubleshooting IT issues.

  5. Data and Information Management: Covers core concepts in data and information management, including database administration tasks.

Cybersecurity and Work Integrated Learning (CTWIL)

ICTC, in collaboration with Microsoft, Digital, and a select group of post-secondary institutions, have joined together to expand the pool of cybersecurity talent in Canada. This program provides women+ and non-binary post-secondary education students with access to mentorship, Microsoft training and certifications, and experiential learning opportunities—one of which is an up to 16-week paid work-integrated learning placement in cybersecurity.
